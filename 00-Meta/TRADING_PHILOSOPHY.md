# HermesForge Trading Philosophy

## Core Approach
HermesForge focuses on **Swing Trading** and **Position Trading** — patient, thesis-driven trades that exploit medium-to-longer term market moves. We are not a scalping or high-frequency system.

## Trading Styles

### Swing Trading
- **Timeframe**: 2-10 days
- **Markets**: US Stocks, Crypto
- **Vehicles**: Stocks (long), Options (debit), Crypto spot
- **Edge**: Technical setups, momentum, catalyst-driven moves
- **Discipline**: Exit if thesis not playing out within timeframe — no holding losers

### Position Trading
- **Timeframe**: Weeks to months
- **Markets**: US Stocks primarily, Crypto selectively
- **Vehicles**: Stocks (buy-and-hold), Options (60-90 DTE, directional)
- **Edge**: Fundamental thesis, macro tailwinds, strong technical trend
- **Discipline**: Written thesis required before entry. Review thesis weekly.

## Market Coverage

### US Stock Market
- Buy-and-hold positions in stocks with strong fundamental or technical thesis
- Options for defined-risk directional exposure (not premium selling initially)
- Focus on liquid, large/mid cap stocks with clear trends

### Cryptocurrency
- Swing and position trades on top-20 crypto by market cap
- Higher volatility → stricter position sizing (0.5% max risk vs 1% for stocks)
- No leverage. Spot only.
- Crypto is a complement to stocks, not the primary focus

## What We Are NOT
- Not a day trading / scalping system
- Not a 0DTE options system
- Not a high-frequency or algorithmic execution system (initially)
- Not a leveraged trading system

## Decision Framework
Every trade needs:
1. **Thesis**: Why are we entering? What is the expected move?
2. **Timeframe**: Swing or Position? Expected hold period?
3. **Invalidation**: What would prove the thesis wrong?
4. **Size**: Calculated per risk rules (1% stocks, 0.5% crypto)
5. **Exit plan**: Target and stop defined before entry

## Evolution Path
Phase 0 (Now): Define rules, research strategies, paper trade
Phase 1: Validate strategies with 30+ paper trades, positive expectancy
Phase 2: Live trading with small size
Phase 3: Scale what works, eliminate what doesn't
