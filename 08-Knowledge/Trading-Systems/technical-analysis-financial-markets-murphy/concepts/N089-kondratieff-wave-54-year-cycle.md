# Kondratieff Wave (54-Year Cycle)

The Kondratieff cycle is an approximate 54-year cycle of economic activity first discovered by Russian economist Nikolai D. Kondratieff in the 1920s. It appears to exert major influence on virtually all stock and commodity prices, and has been identified in interest rates, copper, cotton, wheat, stocks, and wholesale commodity prices. Kondratieff tracked his 'long wave' from 1789 using commodity prices, pig iron production, and wages.
