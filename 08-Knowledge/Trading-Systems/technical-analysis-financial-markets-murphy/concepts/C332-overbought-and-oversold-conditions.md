# Overbought and Oversold Conditions

Oscillators determine when a market is in an overbought or oversold condition. When the oscillator reaches an upper extreme, the market is overbought and vulnerable to a selloff. When the oscillator reaches a lower extreme, the market is oversold and due for a bounce.


## Direct Quotes

> "When an oscillator reaches an upper extreme, it is believed that a market has risen too far and is vulnerable to a selloff." — John J. Murphy

> "When an oscillator reaches a lower extreme, it is believed that a market has dropped too far and is due for a bounce." — John J. Murphy
