# Continuation Head and Shoulders Pattern (Bullish)

In an uptrend, prices trace a sideways rectangular-looking pattern where the middle trough is lower than either of the two shoulders, forming an upside-down head and shoulders. This pattern signals continuation of the uptrend and is confirmed when price closes above the neckline. Because it is inverted relative to the reversal version, it cannot be confused with the reversal head and shoulders pattern.


## Direct Quotes

> "the middle trough in an uptrend tends to be lower than either of the two shoulders" — John J. Murphy
