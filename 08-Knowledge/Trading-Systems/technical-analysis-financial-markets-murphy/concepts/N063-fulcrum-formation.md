# Fulcrum Formation

The fulcrum is a well-defined congestion area occurring after a significant advance or decline that forms either an accumulation base or a distribution top. In a base, the bottom of the area is subjected to repeated tests interrupted by intermittent rally attempts. The fulcrum very often takes on the appearance of a double or triple bottom. The basing pattern is completed when a breakout (catapult) occurs over the top of the congestion area.
