# 28-Day Trading Cycle in Commodities

Most commodity markets have a tendency to form a trading cycle low every 4 weeks (28 calendar days). Translated into actual trading days, the 28-day calendar cycle becomes approximately 20 trading days. This cycle explains the widespread use of 5, 10, and 20 day moving averages and oscillators, as well as the 4, 9, and 18 day variants.


## Direct Quotes

> "most markets have a tendency to form a trading cycle low every 4 weeks" — John J. Murphy

> "The 28 day cycle is based on calendar days. Translated into actual trading days, the number becomes 20" — John J. Murphy
