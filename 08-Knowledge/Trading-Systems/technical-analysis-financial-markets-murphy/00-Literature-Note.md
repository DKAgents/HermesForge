# Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets
**Author:** John J. Murphy
**Ingested:** 2026-07-19
**Notes generated:** 1302

## Summary
Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets by John J. Murphy is the definitive reference on technical analysis, covering chart patterns, trend analysis, indicators, intermarket relationships, and trading system design.

## Core Thesis
Price action discounts all information; studying price history through charts and indicators reveals recurring patterns that give traders a probabilistic edge.

## Key Quotes

> "Technical analysis is the study of market action, primarily through the use of charts, for the purpose of forecasting future price trends." — John J. Murphy, p. 1

> "The author's strong belief is that a full appreciation of the technical approach must begin with a clear understanding of what technical analysis claims to be able to do and, maybe even more importantly, the philosophy or rationale on which it bases those claims." — John J. Murphy, p. 1

> "Since the principles of technical analysis are universal, it wasn't that difficult to broaden the focus to include all financial markets." — John J. Murphy, p. xxviii

> "It has been referred to by many in the field as the 'Bible' of technical analysis." — John J. Murphy, p. xxvii

> "Market action discounts everything." — John J. Murphy, p. unknown

> "The technician believes that anything that can possibly affect the price—fundamentally, politically, psychologically, or otherwise—is actually reflected in the price of that market." — John J. Murphy, p. unknown

> "The whole purpose of charting the price action of a market is to identify trends in early stages of their development for the purpose of trading in the direction of those trends." — John J. Murphy, p. unknown

> "The chartist does not necessarily try to outsmart or outguess the market." — John J. Murphy, p. unknown

> "The chartist knows there are reasons why markets go up or down. He or she just doesn't believe that knowing what those reasons are is necessary in the forecasting process." — John J. Murphy, p. unknown

> "The charts do not in themselves cause markets to move up or down. They simply reflect the bullish or bearish psychology of the marketplace." — John J. Murphy, p. unknown
