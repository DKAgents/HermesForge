# HPI Zero Line Crossings – Buy and Sell Signals

Crossings of the HPI above the zero line are interpreted as buy signals, while crossings below the zero line are interpreted as sell signals. On daily data, the HPI may cross above and below the zero line several times before a longer-lasting buy or sell signal is confirmed. This characteristic means daily signals can produce multiple false or short-term signals before a sustained trend signal emerges.


## Direct Quotes

> "Crossings above zero are buys (B); crossings below are sells (S)." — John J. Murphy

> "This is characteristic of the HPI when used on the daily data as it will cross above and below the zero line several times before a longer lasting buy or sell signal is given." — John J. Murphy
