# Top-Down Chart Analysis: Long-Term to Short-Term

The proper order for chart analysis is to begin with the longest time frame and gradually work toward the near term. Starting with a 20-year monthly chart, then a 5-year weekly chart, then a 6-to-9-month daily chart, and finally intraday charts ensures all data is considered before narrowing focus. Beginning with only near-term charts forces constant revision of conclusions as more data is added.


## Direct Quotes

> "The proper order to follow in chart analysis is to begin with the long range and gradually work to the near term." — John J. Murphy

> "By starting with the big picture, going back as far as 20 years, all data to be considered are already included in the chart and a proper perspective is achieved." — John J. Murphy
