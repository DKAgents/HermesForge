# Weekly Chart Trendlines for Identifying Major Bottoms

Long-term up trendlines drawn on weekly charts connecting multi-year lows serve as major support levels. When price pulls back to test these trendlines, it often marks a significant bottom and potential entry point. Tracking weekly charts allows traders to identify these trendline support levels that would not be visible on daily charts.


## Direct Quotes

> "The early 1998 bottom in General Motors began right at the trendline drawn along the 1995-1996 lows. That's why it's a good idea to track weekly charts." — John J. Murphy
