# Previous Peaks as Future Support in Uptrend

[Paraphrase] In an uptrend, once a prior resistance peak is decisively broken, that level transforms into a support zone. On subsequent corrections, prices tend to find support at or near that prior resistance peak. This is illustrated by the Nasdaq 100 example where a 1997 resistance peak became support during a later intermediate decline.


## Direct Quotes

> "In an uptrend, resistance levels that have been broken by a significant margin become support levels. Notice that once resistance at point 1 is exceeded, it provides support at point 4. Previous peaks function as support on subsequent corrections." — John J. Murphy, p. 82
