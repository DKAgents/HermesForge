# Descending Triangle

A descending triangle is a sideways price pattern between two converging trendlines where the upper line is declining while the lower line is flat. It is generally a bearish pattern.


## Direct Quotes

> "A sideways price pattern between two converging trendlines, in which the upper line is declining while the lower line is flat. This is generally a bearish pattern." — John J. Murphy
