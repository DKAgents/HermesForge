# Moving Averages as Oscillators via Double Crossover

One way to construct an oscillator is to compare the difference between two moving averages. The double crossover method takes on greater significance when viewed as an oscillator. MACD (Moving Average Convergence/Divergence) is one such method that compares two exponentially smoothed averages and is used partially as an oscillator.
