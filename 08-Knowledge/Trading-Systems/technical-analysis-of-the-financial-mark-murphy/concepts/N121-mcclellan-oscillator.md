# McClellan Oscillator

Developed by Sherman McClellan, the McClellan Oscillator is the difference between the 19-day (10% trend) and 39-day (5% trend) exponential moving averages of the daily net advance-decline figures. It fluctuates around a zero line with extremes ranging from +100 to -100. A reading above +100 signals an overbought market; a reading below -100 signals an oversold market. Crossings above and below the zero line are interpreted as short to intermediate term buying and selling signals respectively.
