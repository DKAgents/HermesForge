# Open Interest Definition

Open interest is the total number of outstanding or unliquidated contracts at the end of the day, representing the total number of outstanding longs or shorts in the market—not the sum of both. A contract requires both a buyer and a seller, so two participants create only one contract. Official volume and open interest figures are reported a day late in futures markets and are plotted with a one-day lag.
