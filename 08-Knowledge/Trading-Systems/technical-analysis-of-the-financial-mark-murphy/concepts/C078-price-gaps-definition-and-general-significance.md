# Price Gaps — Definition and General Significance

Price gaps are areas on a bar chart where no trading has taken place. In an uptrend, prices open above the highest price of the previous day, leaving an unfilled space. Upside gaps are signs of market strength, while downside gaps are signs of weakness. Gaps can appear on weekly and monthly charts and are usually very significant when they do.


## Direct Quotes

> "Upside gaps are signs of market strength, while downside gaps are usually signs of weakness." — John J. Murphy
