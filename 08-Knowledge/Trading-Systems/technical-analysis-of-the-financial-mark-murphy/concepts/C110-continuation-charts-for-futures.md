# Continuation Charts for Futures

Because futures contracts have a limited trading life of about a year and a half, technicians must construct continuation charts to study long-range price history. The most common method is to link successive nearest-expiring contracts together, switching to the next contract when the current one expires. This provides price continuity across multiple contract expirations.


## Direct Quotes

> "The technique most commonly employed is simply to link a number of contracts together to provide continuity." — John J. Murphy
