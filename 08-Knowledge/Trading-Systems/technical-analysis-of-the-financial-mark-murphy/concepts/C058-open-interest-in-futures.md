# Open Interest in Futures

Open interest is the total number of outstanding futures contracts held by market participants at the end of the day. Because every long position must have a corresponding short, open interest is counted only on one side (either longs or shorts, not both combined). It is plotted on the chart as a solid line, typically above the volume bars but below the price bars.


## Direct Quotes

> "Open interest is the total number of outstanding futures contracts that are held by market participants at the end of the day. Open interest is the number of outstanding contracts held by the longs or the shorts, not the total of both." — John J. Murphy, p. 42

> "Open interest is marked on the chart with a solid line along the bottom, usually just above the volume but below the price." — John J. Murphy, p. 42
