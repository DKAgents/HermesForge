# Centering the Moving Average

The statistically correct way to plot a moving average is to center it, placing it in the middle of the time period it covers (e.g., a 10 day average placed five days back). However, centering has the major flaw of producing much later trend change signals. Therefore, moving averages are usually placed at the end of the time period covered, and the centering technique is used almost exclusively by cyclic analysts to isolate underlying market cycles.
