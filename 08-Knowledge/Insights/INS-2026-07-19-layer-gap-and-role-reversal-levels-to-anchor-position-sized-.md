# Layer Gap and Role-Reversal Levels to Anchor Position-Sized Stops

## Discovery Summary

C065 establishes that violated support becomes resistance in a downtrend, and EN069 identifies price gaps as discrete support/resistance zones with specific entry and stop rules (sell rally to gap lower end, stop above gap). RG035 completes the chain: once a valid technical level is identified — whether a role-reversed support-turned-resistance or a gap boundary — that level anchors the protective stop, and the stop distance then determines position size within the 5% max-risk / 10% max-commitment framework. The non-obvious insight is that two independent technical structures (role-reversal levels from C065 and gap boundaries from EN069) can converge at the same price zone in a downtrend, creating a doubly-confirmed resistance ceiling that simultaneously satisfies RG035's requirement for a 'valid technical level' for stop placement — and because the level is tighter/more precise when two structures align, position size can be increased while keeping risk constant.

## Trading Implication

In a downtrend, identify where a prior violated support level (C065) and an unfilled overhead gap (EN069) coincide; sell the rally into that confluence zone, place the protective stop just above the gap upper boundary, then size the position so the stop-to-entry distance consumes no more than 5% of total account equity per RG035.
