# Three-Layer Weekly Framework Filters Daily MACD/Stochastic Signals

## Discovery Summary

R142 establishes that MACD and Stochastics crossover systems require weekly-chart trend confirmation before acting on daily signals. N044 provides a concrete weekly trend definition: the 10/40-week moving average relationship determines primary trend direction. C130 adds a third weekly-timeframe tool — price channel breakouts — that can serve as an independent weekly trend confirmation mechanism. Together, these three notes construct a multi-layer weekly filter: a daily MACD or Stochastics signal is only tradeable when (1) weekly 10/40 MA alignment agrees, and (2) price is within or breaking out of a weekly channel in the same direction.

## Trading Implication

Before taking a daily MACD or Stochastics crossover signal, a trader should check that price is above the 40-week MA (for longs) and that a weekly price channel breakout has occurred in the same direction — only then is the daily signal considered trade-eligible.
