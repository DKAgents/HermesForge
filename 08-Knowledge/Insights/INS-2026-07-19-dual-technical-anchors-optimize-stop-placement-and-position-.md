# Dual Technical Anchors Optimize Stop Placement and Position Sizing

## Discovery Summary

C065 and EN069 together provide two distinct but complementary technical levels — violated support-turned-resistance and unfilled gaps — that can be used as precise stop anchor points in downtrends. RG035 then completes the sequence: once these technical stop levels are identified (e.g., sell into a rally toward the old support level or the lower end of a gap), the distance to that stop determines maximum position size under the 5% risk / 10% commitment rule. The non-obvious insight is that gaps and role-reversed support levels often cluster near each other in downtrends, creating a tighter, more defensible resistance zone that allows a closer stop and therefore a larger position size without violating money management constraints.

## Trading Implication

In a downtrend, identify both the violated support level (C065) and any nearby unfilled gap (EN069) as a resistance cluster, place the protective stop just above that cluster, then back-calculate position size using the 5% risk rule from RG035 — the tighter the cluster, the larger the allowable position.
